The 1st International Workshop on Dynamic Scene Reconstruction

Reconstruction of general dynamic scenes is motivated by potential applications in film and broadcast production together with the ultimate goal of automatic understanding of real-world scenes from distributed camera networks. With recent advances in hardware and the advent of virtual and augmented reality, dynamic scene reconstruction is being applied to more complex scenes with applications in Entertainment, Games, Film, Creative Industries and AR/VR/MR. This workshop aims to give an overview of the advances of computer vision algorithms in dynamic scene reconstruction to the target audience and will identify future challenges. The proposed workshop on Dynamic Scene Reconstruction is the first workshop to address the bottlenecks in this field by making the following contributions:

  • One of the primary motivations of this workshop is to bring together leading experts in the field (young and senior researchers with equal representation and contribution from academia and industry) of general dynamic scene reconstruction with the aim of setting out a methodology for capturing a ground truth data set and evaluation framework (as has been done with static 3D reconstruction, recognition and other fields) to help propel the field forward.

  • We will compile and maintain an online comprehensive list of available single and multi-view datasets, open-source software and publications to benefit the research community working with dynamic scenes (includes humans, animals, other non-rigid structures for example trees, waterfalls, and clothing) and to set the foundation for future competitions/challenges in subsequent workshops.

  • The aim of the workshop is to make a quick and significant progress in the field of dynamic scene reconstruction to match the requirements of real world applications (as static reconstruction has already reached) by identifying the challenges and ways to address them through panel discussion between experts, presenters and attendees.
